💰 Money / Expensive
P1 — Clarify what they mean
"OK NAME, no problem. When you say that's _____, what do you mean specifically? Because that can mean many things. It could mean:"
• That's more than I thought it would be.
• That's _____ but I'm willing to pay it IF it gets me their goal.
• That's _____ because I don't have the money.
• That's _____ because I don't have the money, but I'm willing to find it.
"So can you clarify which one is it?"
P2 — Is it worth it?
"Ok. But if it gets you the result you want, can I ask — do you think it would be worth it?"
"So where do you want to go from here?"
P3 — Money aside reframe
"OK no problem. I can see how the investment could be a big step. And getting you from their issue to their goal is also a big step too, right?"
"So money aside — because that's the easy stuff — in your own opinion, do you feel that what we do can get you to where you want to go?"
"Why do you feel it can?"
P4 — The $5,000 test
"So if you had the $5,000 in your hand right now, would you do it?… or would you take a vacation and go to the beach?"
"So is it a case of a little bit of uncertainty around the investment — or is it that you just don't have the investment? Which one is it?"
P5 — Logistics Close
"So would it be fair to say that it's just an issue with the logistics of the investment — meaning, you know we can get you to their goal, but we just need to figure out how, right?"
"If that's the case, would you like me to see how we can break this down into more manageable payments for you?… Would that help you move forward so we can get you to their goal?"
"So we can do 4 payments of $1,500 — or one payment of $5,000 and save $1,000. Which would make more sense for you?"

P2a — Confirm it's 100% what they want

"So why don't we do this — let's arrange a call with you and your partner/spouse where we can go through this together. Because I'm sure if this is a joint decision, they're going to have opinions and questions that you can't answer regarding my program — but I can. Is that fair?"
"So what we'll do is take a small deposit to lock in your spot, and we'll book that call."

🔥 The Analogy — Broken Glass
"Can I share something with you?"

"Imagine you're standing at one end of a 10-foot stretch of broken glass — barefoot. Would you walk across it? Probably not, right? Of course not."

"But now imagine your child is in a burning building on the other side of that glass. Would you walk across it then?"

"Every single person says yes. Because when what's on the other side is important enough — fear doesn't go away, you just stop letting it make the decision."

"Right now, their goal is on the other side of that glass."

"The only question is — is it important enough to you?"
